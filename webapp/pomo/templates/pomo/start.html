What is Pomodoro technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a kitchen timer to break work into intervals, typically 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. Each interval is known as a pomodoro, from the Italian word for tomato, after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo used as a university student. [Wikipedia]

What is Arcadia?

Arcadia is a platform that transforms your study routine into a magical adventure. At its core, it functions like a regular Pomodoro timer: set a timer, and focus. However, what sets Arcadia apart is the immersive experience—each session is accompanied by a story snippet, with new events unfolding before and after the timer. Navigate a map of Arcadia's wizarding school, explore its unique locations, and unlock exciting events as you progress.

Our Mission

TikTok, Instagram and Co. will always be more fun and exciting than studying. But why don't we use the same dark pattern as those apps to make it as close as possible. Our goal is to make studying engaging, productive, and rewarding. By blending gamification with personal development, Arcadia helps you stay focused, achieve your goals, and enjoy the process of learning—one Pomodoro at a time. Our mission is to strike the perfect balance between engagement and focus. Arcadia is designed to motivate you with an enchanting narrative and gamified elements, but never to the point where it distracts from your studies. By combining just the right amount of fun with productivity, we aim to help you stay committed to your goals while enjoying the process.
